Mission

To bridge the gap between rural underserved communities and quality healthcare enabling general health equity and improved wellbeing.

Vision

A world where every girl can manage her menstrual health with dignity, access to resources, and support from her community.

Core Principles

  • Dignity and respect for all beneficiaries
  • Local ownership and sustainability
  • Evidence-informed practices
  • Inclusive, non-judgmental programming

Values

LLF believes in compassion, transparency and collaboration. We partner with local organisations and communities to ensure solutions are relevant, effective and sustained.

Strategic priorities

  1. Scale to multiple communities across Kaduna State
  2. Formalise pharmacy and supply partnerships
  3. Introduce SMS & audio tools for low-bandwidth contexts
  4. Strengthen monitoring, evaluation and learning

How we measure success

We monitor attendance, knowledge change, kit satisfaction and school attendance where available. We commit to transparent reporting back to our partners and communities.

Programmatic pillars

Education & training
Supply & procurement
Monitoring & learning

Community engagement model

We begin with community mapping and stakeholder analysis, then co-design sessions with mothers, teachers and leaders. We build trust through transparent communications and inclusive programme design.

  1. Stakeholder engagement
  2. Community co-design
  3. Peer educator training
  4. Scale & handover to local partners

Case studies & learning

LLF documents learning from every site and shares summaries with partners. Findings inform curriculum improvements and procurement strategies.
