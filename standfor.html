LLF — The Meaning

Lazywings Lifeline Foundation (LLF) embodies our commitment to provide a lifeline for adolescent girls through dignity, education and community-based solutions.

Founding story

LLF was started after founders observed recurring interruptions in girls' education linked to lack of menstrual hygiene resources. Pilots with small community groups evolved into a structured approach focused on sustainability and local ownership.

Approach

Our approach combines:

  • Direct service (kits, workshops)
  • Systems strengthening (pharmacy partnerships)
  • Digital, low-bandwidth learning (SMS/audio guides)
  • Community-driven monitoring & feedback

Outcomes we aim for

  • Reduced absenteeism due to menstrual challenges
  • Improved knowledge and confidence among girls
  • Local economic opportunities through procurement
  • Sustained supply chains via pharmacies and small enterprises

Community-centered values

We prioritize solutions that are respectful, inclusive and designed with — not for — the communities we serve. Local volunteers are trained to take ownership of activities and reporting.

Long-term aspiration

LLF seeks to build a replicable model that can be scaled across states, combining low-cost interventions with strong monitoring systems and local economic participation.

How to join and support

Support can be financial, in-kind (materials, soap), volunteer time or partnership for monitoring and evaluation. Email info@lazywingsfoundation.org to start a conversation.
